How we taught a chatbot to understand website layout

Plain text retrieval is not enough when a visitor asks where the search bar is or which menu holds the pricing link. Those are structure questions, not just content questions.

Most website assistants flatten a page into text and lose everything about layout. That works for product copy, but it fails the moment the visitor asks something operational: Where do I click? Is pricing in the top navigation? Where is the logo? Those answers depend on the structure of the page, not just the words on it.

What 5minBot extracts beyond text

  • Navigation links and their inferred location.
  • Buttons, CTAs, logos, search bars, and dropdown menus.
  • Footer links and page landmarks such as header or main content area.

Those UI facts are turned into natural-language guide text and stored alongside the regular content chunks. During retrieval, position-oriented questions get a boost so the chatbot sees the layout guide first instead of pretending the answer is missing.

The result is simple but powerful: a visitor can ask where something lives on the site and get a direct answer that feels like guidance from a product specialist, not a shrug from a chatbot.
