What happens under the hood

Enough technical depth to build trust, without turning this into an engineering doc

1. Rendered-site crawl

The crawler visits the public website and reads the live page, not just the raw HTML file.

2. Content and layout extraction

5minBot keeps both readable copy and useful UI signals like navigation, buttons, and common page landmarks.

3. Structured retrieval prep

The content is split into smart chunks so the chatbot can retrieve useful answers instead of dumping summaries.

4. Intent-aware replies

Pricing, support, trust, and getting-started prompts are answered in different styles so the conversation feels more helpful.

Plain English

How the chatbot stays accurate

5minBot does not try to “know everything.” It searches the processed site for the most relevant pages, uses those to build the answer, and falls back when the site does not support the question.

Why that matters

That makes the chatbot feel more trustworthy on product, pricing, support, and onboarding questions than a generic AI reply that tries to improvise.

  • Best suited for public marketing and product content.
  • Useful for pricing, trust, and setup conversations.
  • Designed to avoid unsupported claims when the site is missing detail.

Why teams like this rollout

It is fast to test, easy to understand, and gives you a checkpoint before anything goes live on the public site.

What to test first

Start with pricing, setup, support, and “where do I click?” prompts. Those are the questions that show whether the bot is truly useful.
