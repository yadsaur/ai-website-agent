The 100 questions every website visitor asks before buying

Most buyer questions look different on the surface, but they collapse into a handful of intents that need different answer strategies.

Visitors do not ask random questions. They ask the same high-frequency questions over and over: What does this do? How much does it cost? Can I trust you? Will this fit my workflow? What happens after I sign up?

The seven intent buckets that matter most

  • Pricing and cost
  • Product understanding
  • Trust and proof
  • Security and compliance
  • Integrations and technical fit
  • Support and onboarding
  • Getting started and buying

The interesting part is not just retrieval. It is response strategy. A pricing answer should be short and direct. A trust answer needs specific evidence. A security answer needs precision and honesty. A “how do I sign up?” answer should tell the visitor exactly where to click.

Once you model the intent correctly, your website starts sounding less like a knowledge base and more like a sales team that knows what stage the visitor is in.
