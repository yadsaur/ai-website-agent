The buyer questions that really shape conversion

Most pre-purchase questions look different on the surface, but they collapse into a handful of patterns that tell you exactly what the visitor is trying to decide.

Visitors rarely ask random questions. They ask versions of the same few questions over and over: What does this do? How much does it cost? Can I trust it? Will it fit our workflow? What happens after I sign up? If you can answer those moments well, your site starts to feel much more convincing.

The categories that matter most

  • Pricing and billing questions.
  • Product understanding and fit questions.
  • Trust and legitimacy questions.
  • Security and compliance questions.
  • Support and onboarding questions.
  • Getting-started and where-to-click questions.

The important insight is that each category needs a different answer strategy. Pricing should be short and direct. Trust needs specifics. Security needs precision and honesty. Getting-started questions should point to a clear next step. If every answer sounds the same, the assistant feels like a summarizer instead of a helpful product teammate.

That is why SiteCloser treats intent as part of the product, not just part of the prompt. The website assistant should sound like it understands what the visitor is trying to decide, not like it is just reading paragraphs back to them.
